What to do in Nairobi

Nairobi National Park

Located only 7km from the city center, Nairobi National Park is the best place to get up close and personal with wildlife while they remain in their natural habitat. This is the only game reserve this close to a major city center in the world. Some of the popular animals include Lions, endangered Rhinos, Zebras and Giraffes.

Details

Duration: 3 to 4hrs

Fee: Starts for $60

7km from the city center

visit

Giraffe Center

The main purpose of the Giraffe Center is to educate adults and youth alike on their country’s wildlife and environment, as well as give visitors an opportunity to come into close contact with the world’s tallest species, the giraffe. Visitors get to touch and feed the giraffes pellets provided by the attendants.

Details

Duration: 2 to 3hrs

Fee: Starts for $30

7km from the city center

visit

David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust

This is an orphanage project which rescues, rehabilitates, and ultimately releases baby elephants, rhinos, and giraffes back into the wild. You'll have the opportunity to watch the babies being hand-fed by their carers, and enjoying a mud bath or soil dusting. The visit is both entertaining and educational, with one of the keepers giving a fascinating lecture about the project's history and goals, the issues that face elephants in the wild and the practicalities of raising very young wild animals by hand. You'll also be introduced to each of the babies, learning a little bit about their story and personality.

Details

Duration: 1hr

Fee: Starts for $50

14km from the city center

visit

The Alchemist

The Alchemist is Nairobi’s creative event space, filled with a community of entrepreneurs in art, food, drinks, fashion, and music. It is adiverse collective of wacky and determined entrepreneurs in food, fashion, music and more. With food trucks, restaurants, shops, cocktail bars, stages and meeting spaces.

Bomas of Kenya

Bomas of Kenya is a tourist village and cultural center six miles outside of Nairobi. It features replicas of traditional homesteads, or bomas, from 40 of Kenya’s ethnic groups, as well as traditional dances performed in its large auditorium.

Details

Duration: 1 to 2hrs

Fee: Starts for $50

14km from the city center
